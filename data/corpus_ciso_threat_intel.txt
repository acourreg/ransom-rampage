### Key Points
- **Research suggests** that network segmentation, zero-trust architecture, and vulnerability scanning are among the most effective defenses for small fintech infrastructures, potentially reducing lateral movement risks by up to 80% when combined, though implementation costs and complexity may vary.
- **It seems likely that** MFA deployment and access reviews significantly mitigate social engineering and credential-based attacks, with studies showing a 99% reduction in account compromises, but they require user training to avoid friction in operations.
- **Evidence leans toward** proactive techniques like threat hunting and SIEM/log monitoring enhancing detection of ransomware and data exfiltration, though resource constraints in small setups (6-8 nodes) may limit real-time efficacy without automation.
- **Controversy exists around** honeypots and endpoint isolation, as they excel at diverting DDoS and lateral threats but can introduce operational overhead or false positives if not tuned for fintech's high-availability needs.
- **Overall, the evidence suggests** integrating incident response playbooks with these techniques can improve recovery from attacks, but small fintechs should prioritize cost-effective tools to balance security with revenue impacts.

- **Category**: isolation
- **What it defends against**: lateral movement (T1021, T1570), ransomware (T1486), data exfiltration (T1041, T1567)
- **Resources affected when applied**:
  - defense score (node hardening level, 1-10): 8
  - visibility score (log quality / scan reliability, 1-10): 4

- **Trade-offs**: Increases management complexity; potential performance overhead in node communication
- **Real-world example** in fintech/banking: LoanDepot limited a 2024 breach's spread using segmentation, containing it to non-critical areas. In small fintechs, apply between API gateway and core database to isolate payment engine.
